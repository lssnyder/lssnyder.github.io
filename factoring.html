Factoring Polynomials

Factoring is the process of expressing a polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials. It is a fundamental skill used for simplifying expressions, solving equations, and analyzing functions.

Factoring Strategy

  1. Always factor out the greatest common factor (GCF) first.
  2. Check for special products: difference of squares, perfect square trinomials, etc.
  3. For trinomials, look for two numbers that multiply to \( ac \) and add to \( b \).
  4. If there are four terms, try factoring by grouping.

Example 1: Factoring out the GCF

Factor \( 18x^3 - 12x^2 \)

GCF of both terms is \( 6x^2 \):

$$18x^3 - 12x^2 = 6x^2(3x - 2)$$

Example 2: Difference of Squares

Factor \( x^2 - 16 \)

This is of the form \( a^2 - b^2 \):

$$x^2 - 16 = (x - 4)(x + 4)$$

Example 3: Trinomial (a = 1)

Factor \( x^2 + 5x + 6 \)

Look for two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5: \( 2 \) and \( 3 \).

$$x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3)$$

Example 5: Factoring by Grouping

Factor \( x^3 + 4x^2 + x + 4 \)

Group terms:

$$ (x^3 + 4x^2) + (x + 4) $$

Factor each group:

$$ x^2(x + 4) + 1(x + 4) $$

$$ = (x + 4)(x^2 + 1) $$

Summary

  • Factor out the GCF before anything else.
  • Recognize special identities like \( a^2 - b^2 \) or perfect square trinomials.
  • Use the AC method for trinomials with leading coefficient not equal to 1.
  • For four terms, try grouping. Rearranging terms may help.
